Switzerland


Nestled at the base of the iconic Matterhorn, Zermatt is a dream winter destination for travelers seeking snowy adventure and alpine charm. This Swiss village offers world-class skiing, scenic mountain trails, and cozy chalets.

  1. The Matterhorn
    One of the most iconic mountains in the world, the Matterhorn is a must-see. Whether viewed from the town or experienced up close via cable car, its sharp, pyramid-like peak is unforgettable.
  2. Matterhorn Glacier Paradise
    Europe’s highest cable car station (3,883 meters) offers panoramic views of 38 alpine peaks and access to year-round skiing, an ice palace, and a viewing platform above the clouds.
  3. Gornergrat Railway
    This cogwheel railway takes you on a scenic journey to the Gornergrat summit, offering spectacular views of the Matterhorn and the surrounding snow-covered Alps.
  4. Zermatt Ski Resort
    With over 360 km of pistes, Zermatt is a top ski destination. It's linked to Italy’s Cervinia resort, allowing for international skiing across borders in a single day.
  5. Zermatt Village
    A charming, car-free village with traditional wooden chalets, luxury boutiques, and cozy restaurants. Stroll through snow-dusted streets and enjoy authentic Swiss hospitality.
  6. Rosti
    A crispy, golden Swiss potato pancake often served with eggs, cheese, or bacon. Hearty and satisfying.
  7. Valais Platter
    A regional charcuterie board featuring local cured meats, cheeses, and rustic bread. Perfect for sharing.
  8. Swiss Chocolate
    Don't leave without tasting rich, creamy Swiss chocolate. Many local shops offer handmade, artisanal options.
